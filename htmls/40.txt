When there is <span style="color: #0000ff;" data-mce-style="color: #0000ff;">no </span>moon, the <span style="color: #ff0000;" data-mce-style="color: #ff0000;">star-spangled<br></span>sky is a real treat, for the cloudy nights are few<br>and far between, and the flat roofs of the houses<br>easily lend themselves to the studies of amateur<br>astronomers. Among the stars which can be seen<br>in Cairo, but not in the latitudes of London,<br>Boston, or Philadelphia, are Canopus and <span style="color: #0000ff;" data-mce-style="color: #0000ff;">Horologium </span>at the beginning of the year; while the<br>Southern Cross shows about two-thirds of its<br>height in May and June, though the foot of the<br>cross cannot be seen till one goes south of Assouan. Ara and the Centaur are well seen in<br>July, and the whole of the Scorpion shows to perfection; also at the close of the year we have the<br>Crane and the Phoenix.
