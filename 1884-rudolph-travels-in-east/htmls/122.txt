Let us now return from these eloquent descriptions of<br/>
Brugsch Pasha to our own experiences.<br/>
<br/>
Not far from the obelisk is a garden belonging to the<br/>
Khedive. It is an orange grove, well tended, with pleasant<br/>
walks adorned with flowers and a luxuriant growth of<br/>
African plants. The garden, no bigger than many that<br/>
belong to country houses in Europe, is fenced by a low<br/>
earth wall, and stands near a village in the midst of tilled<br/>
fields.<br/>
<br/>
Baron Saurma urged us to beat it for game. As we<br/>
entered the gate, the thought came to me unbidden that<br/>
here we should find at most the pretty African palm dove<br/>
or other southern small birds, but we were soon to be<br/>
taught something different. Several gentlemen placed<br/>
themselves under cover of orange trees laden with fruit,<br/>
near the principal road which crossed the garden. To me<br/>
was assigned the last place near the wall.<br/>
<br/>
