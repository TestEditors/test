Everything in the Valley of the Jordan is prickly. The<br/>
tall grass in spring has long thorns, with barbs which fix<br/>
themselves in the flesh of man and beast; ev'ery tree<br/>
is beset with prickles. It may be imagined how skin<br/>
and clothes fare, and the really cruel sufferings which<br/>
the sport-loving traveller in those regions must endure<br/>
with resignation.<br/>
<br/>
In tree and bush there was the stir of many kinds of<br/>
birds. In this happy tract of land the animal creation<br/>
is closely packed together, and many beautiful, and to<br/>
us novel, species presented themselves. The voice of<br/>
the genuine Asiatic cooing dove was heard everywhere,<br/>
and the tender <span style="color: #0000FF;">turtle-doves </span>flew high in the air above.<br/>
The great Shrike and many other song birds warbled<br/>
amongst the thick shrubs, while quails rose at every step<br/>
from the wild oats; birds of prey, eagles, kites, and falcons<br/>
also abounded. The smaller animals appeared to be amply represented; slender lizards, fat frogs, and insects<br/>
big and little made the place dangerous.<br/>
<br/>
