The next morning we all went to the small but beautiful<br/>
church, where a Franciscan said Mass for the whole French<br/>

gardens of the little French town. The old man showed<br/>

magic, from the apparently unfruitful desert.<br/>
<br/>
The hour for our departure had come. We took leave<br/>
at the quay of Count Lesseps and Herr Zimmerman, to<br/>
whose attention we had been so much indebted on all<br/>
our railway journeys in Egypt; and accompanied by the<br/>
younger M. Lesseps, his wife, and some French gentlemen,<br/>
were soon in motion. The journey was rapid, and the<br/>
dreary scenery was neutralized by interesting and eager<br/>
conversation.<br/>
