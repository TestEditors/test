In the so-called waiting-room we dined. Evening had
already fallen, and our train stood ready. Our chattels
were put into the carriages, and we soon left Abuskar to
proceed on a further expedition. For two hours the two
brothers Saurma and Prince Taxis kept us company.
When we reached the Valley of the Nile and its station,
these three gentlemen left us.

After very hearty leave-takings, we steamed southwards
towards Siut, the next point in our pleasant Nile journey.
We settled ourselves as comfortably as we could in the
carriages, and soon found the sleep our hard work had so
well earned.
